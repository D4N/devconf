Friday Night Pahty!

Join us in your best themed attire for the 2019 DevConf "Star Wars / Hackers" themed party on Friday, Aug. 16 from 7-10PM. Costumes are encouraged, so come dressed as your favorite Star Wars character or let your wackiest 90s hacker aesthetic shine. The best costumes will receive fun prizes!

Remember, tickets to the party are limited so, if you want to come, be sure to collect a ticket at registration either Thursday or Friday morning. The party will feature a DJ and Keytar Bear! Not to mention frozen treats, cookies and ice cream! As your parents used to say, be sure to eat your dinner beforehand so you can enjoy snacks and desserts!

The Fringe!

Join us on Wednesday, August 14th, the day before the main event, for two lead in events! Thanks FOSDEM for the naming of "the Fringe" :).

{{ ('Centos Dojo') }}:

The CentOS and Fedora communities will be holding a Dojo on August 14th, 2019 - the day before DevConf.US begins - at Boston University, in the Balcony Room of the George Sherman Union building.

Checkout more information about CentOS Dojo at Dojo at DevConf.US.

Cloud-Native AI, the open source way

We will be offering two complementary workshops which cover everything you need to get started with implementing and supporting your machine learning workloads on OpenShift. In the morning session, you will deploy and configure a software stack in OpenShift for AI and ML workloads. In the afternoon you will be introduced to the machine learning workflow and see how to solve a concrete problem using machine learning.

While the event is free, you must register to attend. However, given the vagaries of attendance to a free event, please arrive on time so we don't accidentally give away your seat. So register today!!

{{ ('Diversity scholarships') }}:

In an effort to create a more diverse and inclusive event, we offer diversity scholarships that cover travel-related expenses for attendees from outside of Red Hat who are students or are relatively new or returning to full-time work in technology. As DevConf.US is the DevConf for the North America region, these scholarships are only available to people from the U.S. and Canada. We particularly encourage to apply people from underrepresented in tech or underprivileged backgrounds, including women, LGBTQ people, Blacks, Latinos, Native Americans, veterans, people with neurological differences, and people with physical disabilities. We have awarded a number of scholarships for the 2019 event and look for more next year!
